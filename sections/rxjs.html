Higher-Order Observables RxJS

switchMap vs mergeMap vs concatMap vs exhaustMap

▶

Higher-order mapping operators subscribe to inner observables emitted by a source — they differ in how many concurrent subscriptions they allow and whether previous ones are cancelled.

OperatorCancels prev?Concurrent?Best for
switchMapYes ✓No (max 1)Search — only latest matters
mergeMapNoYes (unlimited)Parallel independent requests
concatMapNoNo (queues)Sequential, order-preserving
exhaustMapN/A (ignores new)NoLogin/submit — no double-submit
search$.pipe(switchMap(q => http.get(`/api?q=${q}`)));
submit$.pipe(exhaustMap(() => http.post('/submit', form)));
queue$.pipe(concatMap(a => http.post('/log', a)));
files$.pipe(mergeMap(id => http.get(`/file/${id}`)));
Subjects & Hot/Cold RxJS

Cold vs Hot + Subject types

▶

A Subject is both an Observable and an Observer — it broadcasts to all current subscribers. Cold observables start fresh per subscriber; Hot observables share one execution.
